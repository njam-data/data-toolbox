Data Warehouse


Welcome to the NJ Advance Media Data Warehouse. This page is very much under construction, but is designed to allow reporters easy access to any and all New Jersey data available. There's a lot out there, but it can often be hard to find.

Initially, this page will be a gateway to some of our favorite state data sources on virtually every topic a news reporter could want. We hope to soon be hosting cleaned data for reporter use and add some search functionality to make navigating even easier. We also hope to expand it to include more federal data.

Have a suggestion for how we can make it better? Notice something that's missing? Please e-mail Carla Astudillo or Stephen Stirling on the data desk or hit the "Ask a Question" button at the top of the page. This page can't grow without the help of the reporting community.

Census


Census data can help you in a world of ways, from deepening a local crime story to revealing sweeping changes in the state's demographics.




Datasets


Census Reporter

A terrific and easy-to-use tool for finding basic Census data about any geographical area in New Jersey and the country beyond. Harnesses the power of the Census beautifully and gives you the option of embedding ready-made graphics to boot.


American Fact Finder

This is the Census' primary data digging tool. It has a slight learning curve, but once you get your head around it you can find data going back to 2000 on virtually anything you want. Best for looking at regional data, rather than just one place.


IPUMS

If you really want to dig deep into the Census, IPUMS gives you highly localized data on a broader diversity of topics than any other product available. Has a pretty high learning curve, so best to only use if you want to spend some time tackling something substantial.


Topics Covered

• Population • Income• Race• Ethnicity• Immigration• Migration
• Housing• Commuting• Poverty• Marriage• Veteran Status• Education
• Age• Fertility• Households• Building Type• Language• Property Value

Financial


Several different agencies keep financial data in New Jersey, from budget data to details on the state's banks.



 
Datasets


State Data Portal
The state's main data portal has most of the financial and budgetary information released by the state

Real Estate


Real Estate is one of the most robust data subjects around, and the aftermath of the Great Recession makes it a great topic for research.



 
Datasets


Construction Reporter
The Construction Reporter contains monthly data on all construction and demolition in New Jersey. The data lags a bit but is in good shape. The data custodian, whose contact info is listed on the site, is very helpful.


Foreclosure Statistics
The state Division of Banking releases data on foreclosures in New Jersey.


Zillow Real Estate Data
Zillow is a terrific resource for state and local real estate information. Their data team is incredibly helpful and if you can't find what you need, they'll grab it for you.


Topics Covered

• Foreclosure• Building Permits• Demolition Permits• Certificates of Occupancy• Home Sales• Median Pricing Information
• Estimated Values•Renter Data• Individual Home Pricing
